01

Definition

Alanine Aminotransferase (ALT) — also called Serum Glutamic-Pyruvic Transaminase (SGPT) — is an intracellular enzyme classified under the transferase group (EC 2.6.1.2). It catalyzes the reversible transfer of an amino group from L-alanine to α-ketoglutarate, linking amino acid metabolism to the citric acid cycle.

ALT is predominantly found in the hepatocyte cytoplasm, making it a highly specific and sensitive biomarker for hepatocellular integrity. Elevated serum ALT levels indicate liver cell damage and increased membrane permeability, allowing intracellular enzyme release into the bloodstream.

🔬

Clinical Significance

ALT is the most liver-specific aminotransferase in clinical use. Unlike AST, ALT is not significantly present in cardiac or skeletal muscle, giving it superior diagnostic specificity for liver disease.

Cofactor: Pyridoxal-5'-Phosphate (PLP)

PLP, the active form of Vitamin B6, is covalently bound to a lysine residue in the active site via a Schiff base (aldimine) linkage. PLP acts as an intermediate amino group carrier throughout the transamination reaction. Vitamin B6 deficiency reduces ALT activity.

📌

Clinical Note — Vitamin B6

In laboratories measuring ALT, PLP (Vitamin B6) is added to the assay mixture to activate the enzyme fully, ensuring the measured activity reflects true enzyme concentration rather than cofactor deficiency.

03

Reaction Mechanism

ALT catalyzes a reversible ping-pong (double displacement) transamination reaction in two half-reactions:

Overall Reaction — ALT (EC 2.6.1.2)
L-Alanine  +  α-Ketoglutarate ⇌ Pyruvate  +  L-Glutamate

Cofactor: Pyridoxal-5'-Phosphate (PLP / Vitamin B6)

Step-by-Step Mechanism

1

Half-Reaction 1 — Amino Group Transfer to PLP

L-Alanine binds to the PLP–enzyme complex. The amino group (–NH₂) is transferred from alanine to PLP, converting PLP → PMP (pyridoxamine-5'-phosphate). Pyruvate is released as the first product.

2

Half-Reaction 2 — Amino Group Transfer from PMP

α-Ketoglutarate enters the active site and accepts the amino group from PMP, regenerating PLP. L-Glutamate is released as the second product.

3

Enzyme Regeneration

PLP is fully regenerated and the enzyme is ready for the next catalytic cycle. The reaction is freely reversible under physiological conditions.

⚗️

Metabolic Relevance

Pyruvate, the product of ALT activity, feeds directly into the citric acid cycle (via acetyl-CoA) and gluconeogenesis. This links amino acid catabolism to central energy metabolism. L-Glutamate produced can enter the urea cycle or be deaminated to α-ketoglutarate by glutamate dehydrogenase.

04

Tissue Distribution

ALT is present in multiple tissues, but the liver contains by far the highest concentration, which accounts for its diagnostic specificity.

🏥

Why ALT is Liver-Specific in Practice

Although ALT exists in other tissues, only the liver releases enough ALT into serum to cause clinically significant elevation. Unlike AST (which rises in both hepatic and cardiac damage), ALT elevation with normal AST strongly points to hepatocellular etiology.

Normal Reference Values

ALT Level Interpretation Clinical Implication
< 56 U/L (M) / < 45 U/L (F) Normal No liver cell injury indicated
1–3× ULN (up to ~170 U/L) Mild Elevation NAFLD, mild hepatitis, medications, exercise
3–10× ULN (170–560 U/L) Moderate Elevation Viral hepatitis (chronic), alcoholic hepatitis
> 10× ULN (> 560 U/L) Marked Elevation Acute viral hepatitis, ischemic hepatitis, toxic/drug injury
> 1000 U/L Severe Elevation Acute hepatocellular necrosis, ischemia ("shock liver"), acetaminophen toxicity

ULN = Upper Limit of Normal. Values may vary by laboratory and assay method. Always interpret relative to institutional reference range.

06

Clinical Significance

Serum ALT is the gold standard biomarker for hepatocellular injury. Under normal conditions, ALT is confined within hepatocytes; cell membrane damage increases permeability, releasing cytoplasmic ALT into the circulation.

⚠️

Important: ALT is an Indicator, Not a Function Test

Elevated ALT reflects hepatocyte damage, not liver function loss. Liver function is assessed by different markers: PT/INR, albumin, bilirubin, and coagulation factors. A patient may have severely elevated ALT but preserved synthetic function — or vice versa in cirrhosis.

📊

De Ritis Ratio (AST:ALT)

An AST:ALT ratio >2:1 is characteristic of alcoholic liver disease (due to mitochondrial AST release and relative ALT deficiency from B6 depletion by alcohol). Ratio <1 is more typical of viral hepatitis.

08

Laboratory Measurement

Principle — IFCC Spectrophotometric Method

Coupled Enzymatic Assay (340 nm)
L-Alanine + α-Ketoglutarate →[ALT, PLP] Pyruvate + L-Glutamate

Pyruvate + NADH + H⁺ →[LDH] Lactate + NAD⁺

Monitor decrease in absorbance at 340 nm (NADH consumption) → proportional to ALT activity
1

Sample — Serum or Plasma

Serum (preferred). Heparin plasma acceptable. EDTA plasma should be avoided. Hemolysis interferes (RBCs contain ALT). Stable 7 days at 4°C.

2

Reagent Incubation

Sample mixed with reaction buffer containing L-alanine, α-ketoglutarate, NADH, LDH, and PLP. Incubated at 37°C.

3

Measurement

Rate of NADH oxidation (decrease in absorbance at 340 nm) is measured over time. The rate is directly proportional to ALT activity in U/L.

⚠️

Pre-Analytical Interferences

Hemolysis — falsely elevates ALT (RBCs contain ALT)  |  Prolonged storage at RT — enzyme activity decreases  |  Lipemia — optical interference at 340 nm  |  Intense exercise — transient elevation (muscle-derived)

10

Summary

📋

Key Takeaways

1. Definition: ALT is a PLP-dependent transaminase, EC 2.6.1.2, primarily hepatic and cytoplasmic.

2. Reaction: L-Alanine + α-Ketoglutarate ⇌ Pyruvate + L-Glutamate (ping-pong mechanism).

3. Specificity: ALT is more liver-specific than AST due to limited extra-hepatic sources.

4. Elevation: Indicates hepatocellular damage — NOT functional liver failure.

5. Conditions: Viral hepatitis (highest elevation), NAFLD, alcoholic hepatitis, DILI.

6. AST:ALT ratio (>2): Suggests alcoholic etiology.

7. Measurement: NADH-linked spectrophotometric assay at 340 nm (IFCC method).
